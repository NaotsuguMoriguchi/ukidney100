Abnormalities in mineral metabolism have adverse consequences that go beyond bone to affect the
vasculature and ultimately morbidity and mortality in CKD patients. Both hyperphosphatemia and
vitamin D deficiency are associated with poor outcomes primarily related to cardiovascular events. New
insights have also been made in the control of serum phosphorus in the setting of kidney failure.

In order to address these topics, an expert panel has been convened. Their presentations address
clinical issues in the management of CKD-MBD and also review the physiologic and pathophysiologic
role of FGF-23 in mineral metabolism including the interaction between phosphorus, FGF-23 and clinical
outcomes in CKD patients.

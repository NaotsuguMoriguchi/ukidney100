Recent research has helped to clarify the complex interplay between bones and arteries in
vascular calcification. New epidemiologic and clinical trial evidence confirms the importance of
managing the phosphorus burden in chronic kidney disease (CKD) patients. Recent systematic
reviews comparing the mortality effects of calcium-based versus non-calcium-based phosphate
CKD progression.

In order to address these topics, an expert panel has been convened. Their presentations
explore the interaction of bones and arteries in vascular calcification. They also review the
biologic basis for vascular calcification, the factors that induce and inhibit calcification, the
impact and management of high phosphate levels in chronic renal failure, the evidence
comparing calcium-based and non-calcium-based phosphate binders, and the risk factors that
predict outcomes in CKD patients.
